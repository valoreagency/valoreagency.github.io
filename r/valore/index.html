Where You Are

The positioning is clear and locked, the visual identity is consistent and elevated, and your content library is deeper than most brands in your category ever build. On your own site, you look exactly like what you are: the premium option, not one of many. That is the hard part, and it is done.

Your Opportunities

Consistency, everywhere else.

The brand is this sharp on the site. Off the site, it has not caught up yet, and every gap is a place the premium perception quietly leaks. This is pure upside: the brand is already built, the reach and the consistency just need to catch up to it.

  • Your social accounts were not linked from the site, so the brand felt smaller than it is (now fixed in the footer).
  • Your LinkedIn and older listings still describe an earlier version of the business, so the market reads two different brands depending on where it lands.
  • There is no visible proof high on the site yet, and premium buyers look for proof before they believe premium.

Quick Wins

A week of "make it match."

  • Rewrite the LinkedIn and Google profiles to your current positioning, and retire the old language for good.
  • Put your proof up front: the two case studies and their hard numbers, high on the homepage.
  • Use one identical bio and one message everywhere: site, socials, profiles, email signature.
